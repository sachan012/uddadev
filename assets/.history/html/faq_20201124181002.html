What is a moneyline bet?

A moneyline bet is one of the easiest kinds of bets you can make at a sportsbook. Simply put, it means betting on a specific team to win a game. A game with a moneyline will have a favorite (the team expected to win) and an underdog (the team expected to lose).

What is point/spread?

The point spread is the projected number of points that separate two teams. A game with a spread will have a favorite (the team expected to win) and an underdog (the team expected to lose).

What is Total (Over/Under) bet?

The terms “total” and “over/under” are synonymous when placing wagers. This type of bet is on the sum of the points scored by both teams in a game.

What will be payout for Point Spread and Total bets?

You will get the same amount in return as the amount you bet (minus) any vig. Short for vigorish, the vig is a commission charged by the bookmaker for accepting a wager.
